Mathematical Framework

The diffeomorphism $f: [0,1]^2 \to \Delta^2$ is constructed through the following process:

  1. Cube to Abilities: Transform cube coordinates $(x_1, x_2) \in [0,1]^2$ to competitor abilities using inverse normal CDF: $a_i = \sigma \cdot \Phi^{-1}(x_i) + \mu$
  2. Special Horse: Add a third competitor with adaptive ability based on the racing context
  3. Racing Dynamics: Apply softmax with temperature scaling to convert abilities to winning probabilities
  4. Simplex Mapping: The resulting probabilities form barycentric coordinates on the 2-simplex

The inverse normal transformation concentrates sampling density near the cube boundaries, creating rich structure in the simplex mapping. The mean-adaptive special horse creates competitive balance by adjusting its strength inversely to the average competitor ability.
